𝗦𝘁𝗿𝗲𝗻𝗴𝘁𝗵𝗲𝗻𝗶𝗻𝗴 𝗧𝗶𝗲𝘀: 𝗖𝗦𝗨𝗖𝗖 𝗛𝗼𝘀𝘁𝘀 𝗣𝗮𝗿𝘁𝗻𝗲𝗿𝘀𝗵𝗶𝗽 𝗠𝗲𝗲𝘁𝗶𝗻𝗴 𝘄𝗶𝘁𝗵 𝗜𝗜𝗘𝗘 𝗮𝗻𝗱 𝗔𝗕𝗜𝗘

August 8, 2024 Caraga State University Cabadbaran City (CSUCC) hosted a productive partnership meeting with the Institute of Integrated Electrical Engineers of the Philippines, Inc. (IIEE) and the Association of Building and Industrial Electricians Inc. (ABIE) at the Office of the Campus Director Conference Room last August 8, 2024. The meeting featured insightful discussions led by Professional Regulatory Board Member Jaime V. Mendoza, who provided updates on the REE Board Exam's Table of Specifications. The conversation also explored opportunities for international internships for Electrical Engineering students and industry immersions for our faculty through collaboration with IIEE's network of industries. Additionally, the new requirements for becoming a Professional Electrical Engineer were addressed, along with a review of the impactful extension activities conducted under Oplan Dagitab Brigada Eskwela by CEIT, IIEE, and ABIE. This gathering brought together key figures, including IIEE National Officers, the Board of Governors, and the IIEE-Butuan Agusan del Norte Chapter officers, led by President Engr. Arnel B. Alipao. ABIE Inc. President Rolando Montajes was represented by members of his organization, while CSUCC Campus Director Dr. Deomila Mamaoag was represented by Dr. Erlin S. Rodas, Division Chief for Academic Affairs. Other attendees included CEIT Dean Engr. Blondie T. Sanchez, EE Department Chairperson Engr. Ianlester Romero, CEPRIC Center Chief Engr. Erickson A. Tacda, and faculty members from CEIT. The discussions and collaborations set the stage for the MOU ceremonial signing, marking a pivotal step forward in enhancing educational and professional opportunities for our students and faculty.

August 9, 2024 - Caraga State University (CSU) and the Institute of Integrated Electrical Engineers of the Philippines, Inc. (IIEE) officially signed a Memorandum of Understanding (MOU) on August 9, 2024, during the 26th Northern Mindanao Regional Conference at Almont Inland Resort, Butuan City. This MOU sets the stage for collaborative efforts in research, education, and extension activities, aiming to enhance immersion programs, improve education quality through internships, and drive joint research projects and events. Key attendees included IIEE National President Dr. Florigo C. Varona, IIEE Northern Mindanao Regional Governor Tomas Virtudez, IIEE-Butuan-Agusan del Norte Chapter President Arnel B. Alipao, CSU President Rolyn C. Daguil, CSUCC Campus Director Dr. Deomila B. Mamaoag, CEIT Dean Engr. Blondie T. Sanchez, CEPRIC Center Chief Engr. Erickson A. Tacda, and other distinguished leaders from both institutions.

July 22-26, 2024 - CSUCC Energy and Power Research and Innovation Center (CEPRIC) participated in Brigada Eskwela 2024 conducted by the DepEd Schools of Magallanes District from July 22-26, 2024. This initiative aimed to prepare schools for the upcoming academic year by addressing potential electrical hazards and ensuring a safe learning environment for students. Our team provided essential services, including electrical system assessments, electrical wiring installation and repairs, and other electrical-related services. This effort is part of the CSUCC College of Engineering and Information Technology pre-extension program BUSWAK which stands for 𝘉𝘂𝘪𝘭𝘥𝘪𝘯𝘨 𝘜𝘱 𝘚𝘺𝘴𝘁𝘦𝘮𝘴, 𝘞𝘪𝘳𝘪𝘯𝘨, 𝘈𝘂𝘁𝘰𝘮𝘢𝘵𝘪𝘰𝘯 𝘢𝘯𝘥 𝘒𝘯𝘰𝘸𝘭𝘦𝘥𝘨𝘦 𝘧𝘰𝘳 𝘋𝘦𝘱𝘌𝘥 𝘔𝘢𝘨𝘢𝘭𝘭𝘢𝘯𝘦𝘴 𝘢𝘯𝘥 𝘵𝘩𝘦 𝘓𝘎𝘜 𝘰𝘧 𝘔𝘢𝘨𝘢𝘭𝘭𝘢𝘯𝘦𝘴. The primary goal of this project is to safeguard students from electrical hazards before they return to school, making classrooms a secure and conducive environment for learning. We successfully rendered our services to eight ( 8 ) schools: Magallanes Central Elementary School, Buhang Elementary School, Caloc-an Elementary School, Sto. Niño Elementary School, Dahican Elementary School, Magallanes North Elementary School, Magallanes National High School, and Father Urios Academy of Magallanes Inc. (FUAMI). Our center remains committed to contributing to the improvement of educational facilities and ensuring the safety and well-being of both students and staff.

𝐌𝐑𝐄𝐂 𝐂𝐨𝐧𝐝𝐮𝐜𝐭𝐬 𝐏𝐚𝐫𝐭𝐧𝐞𝐫𝐬' 𝐌𝐞𝐞𝐭𝐢𝐧𝐠 𝐢𝐧 𝐂𝐚𝐫𝐚𝐠𝐚 𝐒𝐭𝐚𝐭𝐞 𝐔𝐧𝐢𝐯𝐞𝐫𝐬𝐢𝐭𝐲

June 7, 2024 - The Mindanao Renewable Energy R&D Center (MREC) gathered representatives from research centers, industry partners, and higher education institutions throughout Caraga to conduct the MREC Caraga Partners' Meeting last June 7, 2024, at the Caraga State University in Butuan City. The meeting was attended by various renewable energy stakeholders in the region, with representatives from Caraga State University and the Mindanao Renewable Energy Acceleration and Coordination Hub (MinREACH). The meeting was also attended by MREC's new partners in the region from various sectors: the Institute of Integrated Electrical Engineers, Father Saturnino Urios University, Agusan del Sur State College of Agriculture and Technology, and several new industry partners. During the meeting, MREC discussed its ongoing projects and presented the opportunities available in the consortium to its partners. This was followed by a thorough discussion of the research challenges and interests in Caraga, where the need for comprehensive renewable energy resource mapping in the region has become apparent. Among the other significant challenges identified during the discussions was the reevaluation of the electrical grid infrastructure and the need to adapt renewable energy applications in isolated communities throughout the region. MREC, a collaborative R&D center on renewable energy, is among the niche R&D centers in the region (NICER) of the Department of Science and Technology, hosted at and chaired by the Ateneo de Davao University through its Center for Renewable Energy and Appropriate Technologies (AdDU-CREATE).

The R&D Directorate has 35 full-time researchers with regular plantilla positions ranging from Research Assistant to Supervising Program Specialist. Majority of the full-time researchers specialize in various fields of agriculture such as crop science, soil science, agricultural engineering, crop protection, agricultural education/extension and animal science. The others are specialists in related fields such as forestry, fisheries, biology, communication, economics, environmental science, and education. These researchers are not only involved in the actual conduct of researches but also provide research support services such as statistical analysis, economic analysis, editing technical reports, undergraduate thesis advising, preparation of visual aids including powerpoint presentation and posters and preparation of technoguides, bulletins, brochures and leaflets. In addition to the full-time researchers, faculty from the colleges are also engaged in research activities whose specialization are under various disciplines.

Technologies

Renewable Energy Systems

Our renewable energy systems include solar, wind, and hydroelectric technologies designed to harness natural resources efficiently. These systems contribute to sustainable energy solutions and reduce reliance on fossil fuels.

Smart Grid Technologies

We develop and implement smart grid technologies that enhance the reliability and efficiency of power distribution. These technologies incorporate real-time monitoring, automated controls, and advanced data analytics.

Energy Storage Solutions

Our energy storage solutions are crucial for balancing energy supply and demand. We work on advanced battery technologies and other storage systems that support the integration of renewable energy sources.

Energy Efficiency Technologies

Our research focuses on technologies that improve energy efficiency in various applications, from industrial processes to residential buildings. These technologies help reduce energy consumption and lower costs.

Featured Projects

Solar Power Installation at CSUCC

We have successfully implemented a solar power installation project at Caraga State University Cabadbaran Campus. This project aims to provide a sustainable and renewable energy source for the campus facilities.

Wind Turbine Research

Our research on wind turbines focuses on optimizing performance and integrating these systems into local energy grids. We are exploring innovative designs and materials to enhance efficiency.

Energy Storage Innovations

We are developing advanced energy storage solutions to support the reliable use of renewable energy sources. Our innovations include high-capacity batteries and other storage technologies.

Smart Grid Pilot Program

Our smart grid pilot program tests new technologies and strategies for improving power distribution and management. This program provides valuable insights into the future of energy grids.
