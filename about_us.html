About the CSUCC Energy and Power Research and Innovation Center

The Caraga State University â€“ Cabadbaran Campus Energy and Power Research & Innovation Center (CEPRIC) was established on March 19, 2019, through Board Resolution No. 46-10 s. 2019 is dedicated to lead transformative research and innovation for a sustainable and resilient energy future in Caraga region and beyond. The center is committed to advancing energy solutions across different domains through a multidisciplinary approach. Positioned as a pioneer in energy innovation in Caraga, CEPRIC will lead research and development initiatives to achieve its objectives. Its scope of research encompasses a wide range of areas, from precise electrical load forecasting and identification of local renewable energy resources to the application of sophisticated software engineering in power systems and the design of smart grids. In addition to its research focus, CEPRIC will serve as a training center for emerging talents in engineering and technology, fostering collaborative efforts in program development, research, and partnerships with other institutions and industries. The center will also play a role in training initiatives, providing venues for student immersion, and serving as a hub for individuals passionate about energy and power solutions. CEPRIC is actively involved in research and development projects with various institutions, extending consultancy services to industries and research institutions within Caraga and beyond.

VISION

To forge a lasting impact on society, industry, and academe, contributing to a resilient and sustainable energy future.

MISSION

GOALS

This center plays a vital role in the long-term goal of Caraga Region and our country to be more efficient and generate clean, renewable, and reliable power even to remote areas.

This center serves as a catalyst to promote electrical engineering, computer engineering and information technology to the advancement of power systems and energy management in Caraga Region.

OBJECTIVES

  • To provide assessment of local residential electrical wirings
  • Promote sustainable energy practices and innovations.
  • Collaborate with industry partners and academic institutions.
  • Provide training and development opportunities for students and professionals.
  • Support the development and implementation of cutting-edge energy solutions.

    • To lead transformative research and innovation, driving the adoption of sustainable and efficient energy solutions that empower communities, foster economic growth and contribute to efficient, clean, and reliable energy future in Caraga region and beyond.

    UNIT PROFILE

    The CEPRIC is under the Office of the Vice President for Research, Innovation, and Extension (OVPRIE) and is managed by a Center Chief officially designated by the University President. With a resolute mission to lead transformative research and innovation, CEPRIC is strategically positioned to address the pressing challenges and opportunities in the energy sector. The center aims to empower local researchers and students specializing in the field of energy and power systems. It will facilitate advancements in addressing the needs of the region through development of innovative research solutions and commercially viable products across various engineering and technology domains. Scholars and researchers engaged in diverse fields like electrical engineering, electronics engineering, computer engineering, and information technology, among others, are encouraged to actively participate in the center's multidisciplinary research and innovation programs. The aim is to support budding researchers in unlocking their full potential in research and innovation.
